Bubble

“Bubble” is a generative music system that utilizes the Monome and touchOSC for control surfaces. It is written in Csound, C, and Bash, and is designed to run on a network of Raspberry Pis. The name and concept of “Bubble” comes from the intuitive and tacticle satisfaction of popping bubble wrap.

The workings behind Bubble are quite simple. When the system starts up, the Monome grid will light up at random places. These lit up squares are then ready to be “popped” by the performer. A single pop corresponds to an algorithmically generated sample created beforehand which is then processed through randomized realtime DSP.

This is a performance utilizing two Bubble systems for two performers. In addition to my system, a glove controller created and performed by Chelsea Southard has also been incorporated into the system.
